the city book, 2022

perfect bound book, cover paper and thread, 25cm x 20cm, 44 pages, 250 gsm

A book about the city and all the chance encounters it provides daily. Inspired by Jerome Robbins' Glass Pieces, a mix of ballet and pedestrian movement, which begins with dancers walking hastily, purposefully, across the stage, intersecting but never meeting. pas de deux (pedestrian) takes the city as a stage and pedestrian crossings as choreography. Each line of thread represents a walking path, the thicker dash on one end marking the beginning and the knot denoting the end. Halfway, we are introduced to our protagonist, the red thread, and then, to the text, Haruki Murakami's "On Seeing the 100% Girl One Beautiful April Morning". Our protagonist recounts walking past a girl that he immediately recognises as 100% perfect for him but not having talked to her. He spends the rest of the story imagining different scenarios and conversations that they would've had. This book is modelled on New York City, but about any city, about the hundreds of urban encounters that we make on a daily basis, and the imagined possibilities of one encounter being the most important one of our lives but that we just don't know it yet. Speckled grey paper, cover stock, chosen to evoke the soiled concrete of sidewalks. Unique book. Printed using Inkjet. Sewn by hand. By Sarah Yeonkyung Choi, creator, designer, printer, and binder.

the punctuation book, 2022

Perfect bound book, text paper, and tracing paper, 18.2 cm x 10 cm, 55 pages, 90 gsm.

Unique book. Poem made from phrases of Helen Mirra's Escritura-a-través, then split into one phrase per page. Pages were assorted randomly then bound. Punctuation scaled for emphasis, and its mirror-image reproduced on the verso as a 'ghost' of the full text. The next punctuation is also included on each page to guide readers who want to read chronologically. This book is an interactive experiment on semiotics.

sammy and suzie drafts, 2023-

Sammy and Suzie are best friends. Sammy and Suzie are hopeless romantics who always seem to choose the wrong person and text-rant about it to each other the next morning. One day, Sammy and Suzie had an epiphany: their conversations about dating in your twenties are way too good to keep to themselves! They decided to create a magazine to broaden the conversation. The first issue touches upon dating apps, STDs, long-distance relationships, interracial relationships, and an exploration of the male socio-sexual hierarchy. Drawings by Stanley Hsia. Design and direction by Sarah Yeonkyung Choi. Ongoing work. Photos shown are drafts. Print is expected for sometime in May.
